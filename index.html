About

I am a Senior Research Scientist at Odin Vision. At Odin, I work on creating the next generation of AI-enabled applications for endoscopy by delivering state of the art AI software and applications to aid clinicians in the detection and diagnosis of disease.

I am also currently a Senior AI Scientist at Verdure Imaging. At Verdure Imaging, I help to create solutions for ultrasound-aided monitoring and diagnosis of scoliosis to enable chiropractors and physical therapists to make treatment decisions more accurately without the use of X-ray radiation.

My research interests include applying artificial intelligence, modelling and software engineering for surgical procedures and medical interventions. It is a core desire of mine to aim to translate my methodologies and innovations to improve surgical navigation, disease diagnosis, and minimally invasive interventions. I have a strong background in the design and development of computer-aided tools for planning and guidance in surgery. Additionally, I have extensive knowledge of medical imaging and image analysis, having worked with CT, MRI, and ultrasound imaging technologies throughout my research career.

I completed my Ph.D. in Medical Imaging, entitled "Medical Image Registration Using Deep Neural Networks", at University College London (UCL) within the Computer-Assisted Navigation, Diagnosis and Intervention (CANDI) Group within the UCL Centre for Medical Image Computing (CMIC) and Wellcome / EPSRC Centre for Interventional and Surgical Sciences (WEISS), under the supervision of Professors Dean Barratt and Yipeng Hu.

I obtained my M.Sc. in Computer-Integrated Surgery in the School of Computing at Queen's University, studying under Professor, and Canada Research Chair in Computer-Integrated Surgery, Gabor Fichtinger. As a member of the Laboratory for Percutaneous Surgery (The Perklab), my research focused in Computer-Assisted Surgery and Guided Interventions. I specialized in the design, development, and use of augmented reality and holographic visualizations for neurosurgical training and surgical planning with custom-built and off-the-shelf devices like the Microsoft HoloLens, receiving the Distinguished Master's Thesis Award for my endeavours. I also completed my Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Computing at Queen's University. In both programs, I graduated with honours distinction. I received numerous prestigious awards for my academic and research excellence, athletic performance, and contributions to the School of Computing and the Queen's University communities.
